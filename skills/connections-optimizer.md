# Skill: Connections Optimizer

## Trigger

Use when growing or optimizing a professional network with intention — identifying who to connect with, what to say, how to maintain relationships, and how to turn a network into a strategic asset for a specific goal.

## Process

### Phase 1 — Audit Current Network

Categorize existing connections:

| Category | Description | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Core** | People you work with regularly, who know your work well | Maintain — these are your advocates |
| **Strategic** | People in positions relevant to current goals but relationship is weak | Invest — regular touchpoints |
| **Dormant** | Once close, now inactive | Re-engage selectively — quick check-in |
| **Peripheral** | Weak ties but potentially useful | Keep low-effort contact — like/comment |
| **Drain** | Relationship is net-negative | Deprioritize |

Goal: most people should be **peripheral** (low cost, broad coverage), with a small **core** (5-15 people) and **strategic** group (20-30 people).

### Phase 2 — Identify Network Gaps

Given your goal (fundraising, hiring, partnerships, job search):

1. **Who do you need to know that you don't?** — specific roles, companies, or sectors
2. **Who in your current network can bridge you to those people?** — 2-hop connections
3. **What events, communities, or platforms concentrate the people you need?**

Gap analysis template:
```
Goal: [specific outcome]
Missing: [type of person / company / role]
Bridge contacts: [people who know those people]
Venues to meet them: [conferences, Slack groups, alumni networks, Discord]
```

### Phase 3 — Outreach Strategy

For each target connection:

**Cold outreach (no mutual connection):**
- Lead with specific, genuine reason for reaching out (their work, a shared interest, a specific thing you want to discuss)
- Make a small ask, not a big one — conversation first, favor later
- 3 sentences max: who you are + why them specifically + what you want

**Warm outreach (mutual connection or prior interaction):**
- Reference the mutual or the prior interaction immediately
- Slightly warmer tone — still focused and specific
- Offer something (insight, intro, resource) before asking

**Reconnecting with dormant contacts:**
- Acknowledge the time gap naturally — don't pretend no time has passed
- Lead with genuine interest in what they're working on
- No ask in the first message — just re-establish the connection

### Phase 4 — Relationship Maintenance System

Staying visible without being annoying:

| Cadence | Action |
|---|---|
| Weekly | Engage with 5-10 LinkedIn/X posts from strategic contacts (comment, not just like) |
| Monthly | Send a relevant article/resource to 3-5 core contacts with a personal note |
| Quarterly | Check in with dormant contacts ("saw your company just did X — congrats") |
| Annually | Year-end note to top 20 contacts — quick personal update, no ask |

## Output Format

- Network audit summary (category breakdown of current contacts).
- Gap list (what's missing for current goal).
- Prioritized outreach list (top 10 with personalized approach notes).
- 3-month maintenance calendar.

## Constraints

- Quality over quantity — a strong relationship with 50 people beats a weak connection with 5,000.
- Every outreach message must be personalized — template blasts erode network trust.
- Do not connect with people solely to extract value — sustainable networks are reciprocal.
