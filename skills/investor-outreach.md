# Skill: Investor Outreach

## Trigger

Use when drafting cold outreach emails to investors, writing follow-up sequences after meetings or introductions, or preparing investor communication templates for a fundraising round.

## Process

### Cold Outreach

1. Identify the investor's focus: stage, sector, geography, check size — tailor every message to their stated thesis.
2. Find a warm connection or relevant portfolio signal (shared founder, portfolio company in adjacent space) — lead with it if it exists.
3. Draft a 4-paragraph email:
   - **Hook** (1 sentence): why this investor specifically, based on their portfolio or public statements.
   - **What we do** (2 sentences): company in plain language — what it does, for whom, and the core insight.
   - **Traction** (1-2 sentences): the most compelling number available (ARR, growth rate, users, key customer logos).
   - **Ask** (1 sentence): specific — a 30-minute call, not "would love to connect."
4. Subject line: concrete and specific — include a traction number or a name-drop if applicable.

### Subject Line Formula

```
[Traction signal] + [What you do] — [Round/Ask]

Examples:
"$50K MRR, 40% MoM growth — raising seed for healthcare AI"
"500K MAU, YC W25 — intro to discuss Series A"
"Portfolio co [Name] suggested we connect — B2B SaaS, $1M ARR"
```

### Email Template

```
Subject: [Traction] — [Category], [Stage]

Hi [Name],

[Hook — 1 sentence connecting to their portfolio or thesis]

[Company name] [does X for Y] — [core insight in one sentence].

[Best traction metric: ARR / growth rate / users / customer logos].

Would love 30 minutes to share more — do you have time [week]?

[Your name]
[Title, Company]
```

**Example:**
```
Subject: $80K MRR, 3x YoY — AI document review for law firms, raising seed

Hi Sarah,

Your investment in LegalOS caught my attention — we're seeing the same
pattern of AI adoption in legal workflows.

DocMind helps law firms review contracts 10x faster using LLMs trained
on case law — we've cut review time from 4 hours to 20 minutes.

$80K MRR, growing 3x YoY, with 12 paying firms including [LogoA] and [LogoB].

Would love 30 minutes next week — are you free Thursday or Friday?

Ahmed Khouly
CEO, DocMind
```

### Follow-Up Sequences

- **Post-intro (within 24h):** Thank the connector, attach deck, confirm ask.
- **Post-meeting (within 2h):** Summary of what was discussed, next steps agreed, any materials requested.
- **7-day follow-up (if no response):** One sentence — add one new data point (new customer, press mention, metric update). Do not resend the same message.
- **30-day follow-up (if still no response):** Close the loop gracefully — "happy to reconnect when timing is better." Preserve the relationship.
- Do not follow up more than 3 times after initial outreach without a response.

### 7-Day Follow-Up Template

```
Hi [Name],

Following up on my note last week — wanted to share that we just
closed [new customer / milestone] since we spoke.

Happy to send our deck if useful.

[Your name]
```

## Output Format

- Full email text, ready to send (no placeholders left unfilled).
- Subject line.
- If multiple versions: ranked by recommended use case.
- For sequences: label each email with its trigger timing.

## Constraints

- Never fabricate metrics, customer names, or funding history — only use what the user provides.
- Do not over-promise on traction or timeline.
- Keep cold emails under 150 words — investors receive hundreds per week.
- Do not use jargon-heavy language or buzzwords without substance behind them.
- If the user provides no traction data, ask before drafting — a cold email without traction numbers is significantly weaker.
