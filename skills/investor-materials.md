# Skill: investor-materials

## Purpose

Draft investor-facing documents — pitch decks, one-pagers, executive summaries, and update memos — with clear structure and compelling narrative.

## Trigger

- Preparing for a fundraising round or investor meeting
- Writing a monthly/quarterly investor update
- Drafting a one-pager or executive summary for outreach

## Trigger

`/investor-materials [type]` or `draft [pitch deck / investor update / one-pager] for [context]`

## Document Types

### Pitch Deck Structure

```
1. Title slide — company name, tagline, presenter
2. Problem — specific, quantified pain point
3. Solution — what you do, and why now
4. Product — demo screenshot or flow (show, don't describe)
5. Market — TAM/SAM/SOM with sources
6. Business model — how you make money
7. Traction — key metrics, growth rate, logos
8. Team — relevant experience, why this team
9. Ask — how much, what it's for, timeline to next milestone
10. Appendix — unit economics, competitive landscape, roadmap
```

### One-Pager Structure

```
[Header: Logo | Name | Tagline | Contact]
Problem: [1–2 sentences]
Solution: [1–2 sentences + screenshot or diagram]
Traction: [Key metrics in 3–5 bullet points]
Market: [TAM and target segment]
Team: [Names and 1-line bios]
Ask: [$X for Y milestone]
```

### Investor Update (Monthly/Quarterly)

```markdown

**TL;DR**: [1–2 sentence summary of the period]

### Highlights
- ...

### Key Metrics
| Metric | This Period | Last Period | Change |
|--------|------------|-------------|--------|

### Challenges
- ...

### Asks
- [Specific intros, advice, or help needed]

### Next 30/90 Days
- ...
```

## Writing Principles

- **Lead with traction** — investors fund businesses, not ideas. Put real numbers first.
- **Be specific** — "200 enterprise customers paying $50K ARR" beats "strong customer base".
- **Honest about challenges** — investors respect founders who know their risks.
- **One ask, clearly stated** — amount, valuation cap (if applicable), use of funds, timeline.
- **Short** — pitch deck: 10–12 slides. One-pager: 1 page. Update: fits in one email.

## Safe Behavior

- Drafts only — does not send to investors or submit to any platform.
- Financial projections must be clearly labeled as projections/estimates, not facts.
- Does not fabricate metrics or traction numbers — uses placeholders like `[INSERT ARR]`.
